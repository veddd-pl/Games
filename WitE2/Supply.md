In **Gary Grigsby’s War in the East 2 (WitE2)**, supply is the "game within the game." It is arguably the most complex and realistic logistical simulation in wargaming. If your units aren't supplied, they lose movement points, combat effectiveness, and eventually disintegrate.

The system follows a specific **"Pull" hierarchy** that moves supplies from your national factories down to the individual soldier's hands.

---

## 🏗️ The Logistical Hierarchy
Supply flows through four distinct stages. If any link in this chain is broken, the unit starves.

1.  **National Strategic Source**: Supplies and fuel are produced in your home cities (like Berlin or Moscow).
2.  **The Rail Network**: This is the backbone. Supplies move via rail to **Regular Depots**. 
3.  **The Depot**: This is a stockpile location (usually a city or rail hex). It "pulls" freight from the rail line.
4.  **The HQ & Unit**: The Combat Unit pulls supplies from its assigned **HQ**, which in turn pulls from the nearest **Depot** using trucks or horse-drawn wagons.



---

## 🚛 The "Truck vs. Horse" Factor
The distance from a **Depot** to a **Unit** is measured in **MP (Movement Points)**, not just hexes. 

* **0–10 MP from Depot**: The unit is in "Golden" supply. High initiative and full refit capability.
* **10–25 MP from Depot**: Supply starts to drop. Trucks are working hard and wearing out.
* **25+ MP from Depot**: Severe penalties. You are likely outrunning your logistics.

> **Pro Tip:** The weather (Mud/Rasputitsa) drastically increases the MP cost of hexes, meaning a depot that was "close" in the summer becomes "impossible" in the autumn.

---

## ⚙️ Key Mechanics to Manage

### 1. Freight
Freight is the "currency" of logistics. It represents a generic ton of "stuff" (food, ammo, parts). Once freight reaches a depot, the unit converts it into what it specifically needs.

### 2. Depot Management
You have significant control over depots. You can:
* **Set Priority (0-4)**: A Priority 4 depot will aggressively pull freight from the rail net, while Priority 0 will send its freight elsewhere.
* **Build/Expand**: You can manually create depots on rail hexes to shorten the "last mile" to the front.

### 3. Rail Usage & Gauge
* **The Axis Struggle**: As the Germans push East, they must convert Soviet "Broad Gauge" rail to European "Standard Gauge." Your advance is limited by how fast your **FBD (Construction)** units can flip these rails.
* **Rail Capacity**: There is a limit to how much freight your entire national rail net can move per turn.

### 4. The Truck Pool
Every time a unit pulls supply, it uses trucks from the **National Transport Pool**. 
* If you drive through mud or over long distances, your trucks break down (**Attrit**). 
* Once your truck pool is empty, your army stops moving, regardless of how much ammo is sitting in Berlin.

---

## 📈 How to Check Your Supply Status
The best way to learn is to use the **Map Modes**:
* **8 Key**: Shows the **Supply Grid**. It colors hexes based on how well-supplied they are from the nearest depot.
* **9 Key**: Shows the **Rail Usage**. Useful for spotting bottlenecks in your network.
